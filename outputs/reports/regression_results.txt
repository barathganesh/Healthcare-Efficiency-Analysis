SECOND-STAGE REGRESSION ANALYSIS — NON-SAFETY-NET HOSPITALS
Updated Model with Systematic Variable Selection (20 variables)
================================================================================

METHODOLOGY
───────────
After computing hospital-level DEA efficiency scores (input-oriented VRS),
we ran second-stage regressions to identify determinants of efficiency.

Variable selection process:
  1. Inventoried all 635 columns → 608 numeric candidates
  2. Filtered to 191 variables with ≥70% non-missing coverage
  3. Removed duplicate _min/_max/_wtd triplicates → 133 unique variables
  4. Correlation analysis: identified massive multicollinearity (65+ pairs |r|>0.90)
  5. Selected 1 representative per cluster + theoretically motivated additions
  6. Ran 6 progressive models (A→F) to find optimal variable set

PROGRESSIVE MODEL COMPARISON
─────────────────────────────
  Model A (Original 8 vars):      R² = 0.496  N = 41,809  (baseline)
  Model B (+3 financial):          R² = 0.522  N = 39,106  (+0.026)
  Model C (+3 staffing/assets):    R² = 0.522  N = 38,944  (+0.000)
  Model D (+3 HCAHPS quality):     R² = 0.577  N = 36,106  (+0.055)
  Model E (+3 outcomes/CMI):       R² = 0.665  N = 25,590  (+0.088) ← SELECTED
  Model F (+6 kitchen sink):       R² = 0.665  N = 25,590  (+0.000)

Key insight: HCAHPS patient experience (+5.5%) and clinical outcomes/CMI (+8.8%)
are the biggest drivers of explained variance. Adding more variables beyond 20
provides zero additional explanatory power.

KEY FINDINGS (OLS COEFFICIENTS)
───────────────────────────────

  POSITIVE effects on efficiency (higher score):
    discharges_k     +3.565***  More discharges → higher efficiency
    revenue_m        +0.009***  Higher revenue → higher efficiency
    margin_pct       +0.100***  Better margins → higher efficiency
    medicaid_pct     +0.026*    Higher Medicaid share → slightly higher efficiency
    recommend_v      +8.752***  Higher patient recommendation → higher efficiency

  NEGATIVE effects on efficiency (lower score):
    beds             -0.136***  More beds → lower efficiency (overcapacity)
    cost_m           -0.070***  Higher costs → lower efficiency
    ccr              -25.97***  Higher cost-to-charge ratio → much lower efficiency
    is_teaching      -1.464***  Teaching hospitals → 1.5 pts less efficient
    is_rural         -1.087***  Rural hospitals → 1.1 pts less efficient
    overall_hcahps   -8.197***  Higher overall HCAHPS → lower efficiency (counterintuitive*)
    clean_score_v    -5.710***  Higher cleanliness → lower efficiency
    hf_readm         -27.92***  Higher HF readmission → much lower efficiency
    pn_mort          -12.61***  Higher PN mortality → lower efficiency
    cmi              -3.683***  Higher case mix → lower efficiency (sicker patients)

  NOT SIGNIFICANT:
    wage_cost_m      -0.004     p=0.328
    cash_reserves_m  +0.001     p=0.181
    fte_employees    -0.000     p=0.119

  * The negative HCAHPS overall score / positive recommend score reflects
    multicollinearity among HCAHPS sub-scales. Net effect of patient experience
    is positive (recommend dominates).
