================================================================================
TOP 5 SUCCESS FACTORS (RANKED BY STRENGTH)
================================================================================

#1. PATIENT EXPERIENCE EXCELLENCE (Correlation: 0.556) - STRONGEST
    Successful: 2.69/3.0 vs Unsuccessful: 2.57/3.0
    8.8% higher satisfaction = #1 predictor of success
    
    What they do:
    • Dedicated patient experience department
    • Real-time feedback systems
    • Empowered staff to resolve issues
    • Clear communication/transparency
    • Complaint resolution = learning
    • Patient navigation services

#2. READMISSION PREVENTION (Correlation: -0.173)
    Successful: 19.2% vs Unsuccessful: 20.8%
    7.7% lower readmissions
    
    What they do:
    • Discharge planning starts at admission
    • Care coordination post-discharge
    • Early follow-up for high-risk patients
    • Medication management education
    • Primary care physician connection
    • Early intervention for complications

#3. CLINICAL RISK MANAGEMENT (Correlation: -0.162)
    Better clinical governance = success
    
#4. HEART FAILURE READMISSION (Correlation: -0.158)
    Successful: 23.1% vs Unsuccessful: 25.3%
    8.7% lower HF readmissions

#5. PNEUMONIA READMISSION (Correlation: -0.139)
    Successful: 17.8% vs Unsuccessful: 19.4%
    8.3% lower PN readmissions

CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS - REMEMBER THESE
================================================================================

1. PATIENT EXPERIENCE IS #1
   • Highest correlation (0.556)
   • Not optional; foundational
   • ROI is positive

2. SMALL = ADVANTAGE
   • 48.9% vs 25.6% success rates
   • Maintain small-hospital culture at scale
   • Reduce bureaucracy, increase empowerment

3. READMISSIONS ARE WINNABLE
   • Every 1% reduction = better margins AND outcomes
   • Systematic approach with dedicated resources
   • Strong ROI on prevention

4. QUALITY & EFFICIENCY ALIGNED
   • Not a tradeoff; they reinforce each other
   • Better care → fewer readmissions → better margins
   • Better experience → staff retention → efficiency

5. CULTURE BEATS TECHNOLOGY
   • Process + People > Systems + Software
   • Technology enables but culture drives
   • Leadership visibility and empowerment matter most
