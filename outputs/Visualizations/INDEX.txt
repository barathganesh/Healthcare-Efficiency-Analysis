01  VALUE FRAMEWORK SCATTER
    File: 01_value_framework.png
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    What it shows:
      Every hospital plotted on Clinical Quality (Y) vs
      Equity Contribution / Medicaid Share (X), colored by
      Hero (green), Middle (grey), and Struggler (red).

    Key takeaway:
      Heroes cluster in the upper-right (high quality + high
      equity), while Strugglers concentrate at lower quality.
      This visualizes the paper's Value = Quality + Equity
      framework.

    Data source: merged_hospital_data.parquet (4,104 hospitals)
    Slide use: Opening/framework slide

03  STRESS TEST — BEFORE vs AFTER OPTIMIZATION
    File: 03_stress_test_before_after.png
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    What it shows:
      Grouped bar chart showing hospitals at risk of closure
      and negative margins under 4 Medicaid/Medicare cut
      scenarios, comparing outcomes WITH vs WITHOUT
      optimization.

    Key takeaway:
      Optimization dramatically reduces closure risk and
      negative-margin hospitals across all scenarios.
      E.g., under 10% Medicaid cut, closures drop from 35
      to 6 with optimization.

    Data source: stress_test_summary.csv
    Slide use: Stress test results / policy resilience slide

07  MARGIN DISTRIBUTION — BEFORE vs AFTER OPTIMIZATION
    File: 07_margin_before_after.png
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    What it shows:
      Overlapping KDE density plots of operating margin for
      232 Struggler hospitals, before (baseline) and after
      (post-optimization).

    Key takeaway:
      Median margin shifts from 3.1% to 19.6% after
      optimization. The entire distribution moves right,
      virtually eliminating negative-margin hospitals.

    Data source: struggler_optimization_prescriptions.csv
    Slide use: Financial transformation / "proof it works" slide

08  ML FEATURE IMPORTANCE — TOP 15
    File: 08_ml_feature_importance.png
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    What it shows:
      Horizontal bar chart of top 15 features from the
      Random Forest + Gradient Boosting classifier (99.4%
      accuracy). Green bars = higher value → Hero;
      Red bars = lower value → Hero.

    Key takeaway:
      Revenue-to-Cost Ratio, Revenue per Discharge, and
      Annual Discharges are the strongest predictors.
      Readmission/mortality rates also matter — confirming
      that Heroes excel at both finance and quality.

    Data source: ml_feature_importance.csv
    Slide use: ML methodology / "what drives performance" slide
